**UEFN Curriculum Master Guide**

Teaching Game Design and Development with Unreal Editor for Fortnite

High School Edition  |  Epic Games  |  June 2026

# **About This Curriculum**

This curriculum introduces high school students to professional game design and development using UEFN (Unreal Editor for Fortnite). Students learn world-building, visual production, game mode design, programming with Verse, and optimization — culminating in a published Fortnite island.

| Audience: Grades 9–12 | Duration: 4–6 weeks | Prerequisites: None |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Tools Required: UEFN (free) | Platform: Windows PC | Student accounts: Free at epicgames.com |

## **Learning Outcomes**

By the end of this curriculum, students will be able to:

* Design and build a 3D game world using professional tools  
* Apply game design principles including goals, rules, feedback, and balance  
* Implement game mechanics using UEFN Devices and channel wiring  
* Write functional Verse code to create custom gameplay logic  
* Optimize a game for performance and publish it to a real audience  
* Iterate based on playtesting feedback

# **Recommended Teaching Sequence**

## **Week 1 — Getting Started**

* Day 1: Show Deck 1 slides (Getting Started). Students create Epic accounts and install UEFN.  
* Day 2–3: Students work through Step-by-Step Guide 1 (Your First UEFN Island). Play Video 1 and Video 2 as intros.  
* Day 4–5: Students continue building their island. Introduce terrain sculpting, props, and sky atmosphere.  
* End of week: Students publish their island and share island codes with the class.

## **Week 2 — Visual & Audio Production**

* Day 1: Show Deck 2 slides (Visual & Audio). Discuss materials, lighting, and sound design.  
* Day 2–3: Students apply materials and lighting to their existing island.  
* Day 4–5: Add ambient sounds, particle effects, and a post-process volume. Final polish.

## **Week 3 — Game Mode Design**

* Day 1: Show Deck 3 slides (Game Mode Design). Play Video 3\. Discuss goals, rules, and feedback.  
* Day 2: Students fill out a game design doc (goal, rules, win condition) before opening UEFN.  
* Day 3–4: Students work through Step-by-Step Guide 3, placing and configuring devices.  
* Day 5: Cross-playtest — each student plays a classmate’s game and gives structured feedback.

## **Week 4 — Verse Programming**

* Day 1: Show Deck 4 slides (Verse Deep Dive). Play Video 4\. Distribute Verse Cheat Sheet.  
* Day 2: Students work through Step-by-Step Guide 2 (Your First Verse Script).  
* Day 3–4: Students add a custom Verse mechanic to their game mode (score tracker or round timer).  
* Day 5: Code review — pairs read each other’s code and explain what it does.

## **Week 5 — Optimization & Publishing**

* Day 1: Show Deck 5 slides (Optimization & Publishing). Play Video 5\.  
* Day 2: Students profile their islands using stat fps and the GPU Visualizer.  
* Day 3: Fix the top performance issue identified in profiling.  
* Day 4: Final playtesting and polish. Students complete pre-publish checklist.  
* Day 5: All students publish their final islands. Share island codes for a class-wide play session.
